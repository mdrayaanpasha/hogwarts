The Ancient Foundations

"Draco Dormiens Nunquam Titillandus"

Hogwarts, a beacon of magical education, was founded in the Highlands of Scotland during the 9th or 10th century by the four greatest witches and wizards of the age: Godric Gryffindor, Rowena Ravenclaw, Helga Hufflepuff, and Salazar Slytherin.

Born from a need to safeguard young magical talents from Muggle persecution, Hogwarts became a sanctuary of learning. Legend whispers that Rowena Ravenclaw, guided by a dream of a warty hog leading her to a cliff by a lake, bestowed upon the school its peculiar yet iconic name.

For centuries, Hogwarts has been the heart of wizarding education in Great Britain and Ireland, its location shrouded in powerful enchantments, forever hidden from the uninitiated eyes of Muggles and rival magical institutions alike.
